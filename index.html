Palm Care Guide

Planting

Dig a hole at least twice the width of the root ball and only as deep as needed for proper crown height. In sandy sites, blend in quality backfill thoughtfully, add bone meal where appropriate, mound a berm to direct irrigation, and mulch to retain moisture while keeping mulch away from the trunk.

Watering

Water deeply so roots grow downward and outward. A common rhythm is about three times per week in the summer and about twice per week in the winter, with adjustments for soil type, heat, wind, and plant size.

Fertilizing

Use a palm-specific granular fertilizer, fertilizer spikes when suitable, and trace minerals to support strong color and healthy fronds. Feeding should be consistent but never excessive.

Queen Palm Care

Queen palms benefit from balanced watering, sufficient potassium and manganese, and well-drained soil. Watch for frizzle top, a nutrient deficiency that can appear as distorted fronds. If soil pH is too alkaline, a soil program may be recommended, and in certain treatments professionals may use muriatic acid carefully and only with proper guidance.

Warning

Avoid overwatering, compacted planting holes, and trunk damage from string trimmers or mulch piled too high. When in doubt, confirm plant-specific needs before making changes to your irrigation schedule.

Best Practices Summary

1. Choose the right location for sun, drainage, and mature spread.
2. Water deeply and adjust by season, size, and soil.
3. Feed with palm-safe fertilizer and monitor for deficiencies.
