Palm Care Guide

Palm Care Guide

Beautiful palms start with proper planting, thoughtful watering, and the right nutrients for the Phoenix climate.

Table of Contents

Planting Watering Guidelines Fertilizing Queen Palm Special Care

Planting

Choose a planting site with full sun and well-draining soil. Dig a hole wider than the root ball, set the palm at grade, and backfill gently while watering in layers to remove air pockets.

Tip: Avoid planting too deeply; the root initiation zone should remain visible and breathable.

Watering Guidelines

Spring: Water deeply but allow the soil surface to dry slightly between cycles. Summer: Increase frequency during high heat and wind to prevent stress and leaf burn.

Fall and Winter: Reduce watering as temperatures drop, especially for established palms, to prevent overwatering and root issues.

Consistency is more important than volume. Deep, infrequent irrigation encourages strong root systems.

Fertilizing

Use a slow-release palm fertilizer designed with micronutrients, including manganese and magnesium, to support healthy fronds and steady growth.

Warning: Avoid generic lawn fertilizers near palms; they can cause nutrient imbalance over time.

Queen Palm Special Care

Queen Palms are stunning but can show signs of manganese deficiency if not fed correctly. Look for frizzle top, a condition where new fronds emerge weak, stunted, or distorted.

To help prevent this, maintain regular palm nutrition and ensure the planting area drains well. If symptoms appear, act quickly with a palm-specific treatment and consult a nursery professional.
